GRUNGE

Interviewer: Hey Willy! First off, can you tell us a bit about yourself?

Willy: Hey! I’m Willy Kekae, a 19-year-old from the outskirts of Mokopane, Limpopo, South Africa. I’m 6’1, a bit of a twink energy, and I like to think of myself as a mix of creative and analytical — modeling, coding, statistics, arts, and entrepreneurship. I’m all about carving out my own lane.

Interviewer: That’s quite the mix! What drives you to juggle all these different passions?

Willy: Honestly, it’s curiosity and ambition. I’ve always wanted to push boundaries and see how far I can go. I enjoy the creative freedom of modeling and directing, but coding and entrepreneurship keep my mind sharp. I want to master both worlds.

Interviewer: Wow, so where do you see yourself in five years?

Willy: I see myself as “big,” but not just in fame — I mean impactful. I want to be someone who inspires through multiple mediums — whether it’s digital content, tech solutions, or creative projects. I’m not there yet, but I’m grinding every day to reach that vision.

Interviewer: What has been your biggest challenge so far?

Willy: Honestly, balancing everything. Sometimes you feel pulled in so many directions. But I’ve realized that each passion complements the other — the creativity fuels my coding projects, and the discipline from coding fuels my creative work.

Interviewer: That’s a unique perspective. If you could give one piece of advice to young people trying to chase multiple paths, what would it be?

Willy: Don’t limit yourself. Society wants to put people in boxes, but your strengths can span different areas. Be unapologetic about what excites you, and let your journey be your proof.

Interviewer: Lastly, what’s something most people don’t know about Willy?

Willy: I love taking risks and exploring the unconventional. People see the polished end product, but they don’t always see the late nights, the failed experiments, or the times I doubted myself. That’s all part of the growth, and I embrace it.

End of interview.
